     As an artist, I receive my inspiration from the cartoons I have seen throughout the years as well as the new ones that my daughter enjoy. Capturing the interests of young minds to relate to the characters and allowing their minds to see the story. I like to start with images of animals then I add details to make them come alive. Sometimes I use some simple items that people might collect and then I create the owner of the item. This allows me to create more characters that may be included in the story.

 

     For instance, I like to imagine my characters coming from a different world. I would like for children to become intrigued with my cartoons, so they can somehow relate to the story. My work is created with the idea of it being fantasy, so I can create fictional stories. Growing up can be hard for children so when they find a character to connect with it gives them a sense of having an imaginary friend something to relate to.
